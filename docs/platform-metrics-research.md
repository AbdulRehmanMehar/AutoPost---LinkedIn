# Platform Metrics Research & Interpretation Guide

## Overview

This document summarizes research on what each post metric means on each platform (LinkedIn, Facebook, Twitter/X, Instagram) and how to interpret metric combinations to understand content performance.

---

## LinkedIn Metrics

### Core Metrics

| Metric | Definition | What It Indicates |
|--------|------------|-------------------|
| **Impressions** | Times post appeared in feeds | Algorithm distribution / reach |
| **Unique Views** | Unique signed-in members who saw post | Actual unique audience |
| **Clicks** | Clicks on content, company name, or logo | Interest / intent |
| **Reactions** | Likes, celebrates, supports, etc. | Basic engagement |
| **Comments** | Number of comments | Conversation / resonance |
| **Reposts** | Times post was shared | Value / shareability |
| **Engagement Rate** | (interactions + clicks + followers) / impressions | Overall content performance |
| **CTR** | clicks / impressions | Content-to-action conversion |

### LinkedIn Algorithm Signals (Weighted Value)
- **Likes**: 1x weight (baseline)
- **Comments**: 4x weight (strong signal)
- **Shares/Reposts**: 6x weight (strongest signal)
- **Clicks**: 2x weight

### What Metric Combinations Mean on LinkedIn
- **High impressions + Low engagement**: Hook worked but content disappointed
- **High comments + High shares**: Content sparked valuable discussion
- **High engagement rate (>2%)**: Content is outperforming average
- **High likes, low comments**: Content is "nice" but not thought-provoking
- **High reposts**: Content has universal value beyond your network

### LinkedIn Benchmarks
- Average engagement rate: ~2%
- Typical reach: ~15% of followers
- Good CTR: 0.1%+

## Facebook Metrics

### Core Metrics

| Metric | Definition | What It Indicates |
|--------|------------|-------------------|
| **Reach** | People who saw content from your page | Unique audience size |
| **Impressions** | Total times content was displayed | Content visibility |
| **Post Engagement** | Reactions + comments + shares | Overall interaction level |
| **Link Clicks** | Clicks on links in posts | Call-to-action effectiveness |
| **Video Views** | Number of video plays | Video content performance |
| **Page Virality** | % of times content displayed from reactions/comments/shares | Viral potential |

### Facebook Algorithm Signals
- **Likes**: 1x weight
- **Comments**: 3x weight
- **Shares**: 5x weight (most valuable)
- **Reactions (Love, Wow, Haha)**: 1.5x weight (more emotional = more valued)

### What Metric Combinations Mean on Facebook
- **High reach, low engagement**: Content lacks stickiness
- **High engagement + multiple reaction types**: Emotionally resonant content
- **High comments**: Community-building content
- **High shares**: Universally valuable / entertaining content
- **Low reach**: Algorithm not favoring content (try video/images)

### Facebook Benchmarks
- Average engagement rate: ~0.09% (very low)
- Typical organic reach: ~5.2% of followers
- Content type reach: Video > Photos > Links > Text

### Key Insight from Research
> "If the engagement is high but the reach and impression numbers are low (or vice versa), we don't rush to label the content a success or a failure. Instead, we use these insights to refine our strategy."

## Twitter/X Metrics

### Core Metrics

| Metric | Definition | What It Indicates |
|--------|------------|-------------------|
| **Impressions** | Times tweet appeared on feeds | Content distribution |
| **Engagements** | Total interactions (likes, retweets, replies, clicks) | Overall engagement |
| **Engagement Rate** | Engagements / Impressions | Content resonance |
| **Retweets** | Times tweet was shared | Viral potential / value |
| **Replies** | Direct responses to tweet | Conversation generation |
| **Likes** | Heart reactions | Basic acknowledgment |
| **Profile Visits** | Clicks to your profile | Authority/interest building |
| **Link Clicks** | Clicks on links in tweets | CTA effectiveness |
| **Detail Expands** | Clicks to see full tweet/thread | Curiosity generation |

### Twitter Algorithm Signals
- **Likes**: 1x weight
- **Replies**: 3x weight (drives algorithm)
- **Retweets**: 4x weight (reach multiplier)
- **Clicks**: 2x weight

### What Metric Combinations Mean on Twitter
- **High impressions + low engagement**: Content not compelling enough to act on
- **High retweets**: Content is highly shareable, viral potential
- **High replies**: Content sparked debate/conversation
- **High profile visits**: You're building authority/curiosity about you
- **High link clicks**: Strong value proposition and CTA

### Twitter Benchmarks
- Median engagement rate: 2.15%
- Best posting times: 9am-11am weekdays
- Best days: Monday-Wednesday
- Video posts tend to perform best

### Metric Meanings from Research
- **Reposts**: "A sign of value. Someone found your tweet valuable enough to share with their audience."
- **Likes**: "A sign of appreciation. Your tweet resonated with someone else."
- **Link clicks**: "A sign of relevance. Someone found your content relevant enough to head over to your website."
- **Detail expands**: "A sign of curiosity. A user clicked on your tweet to see more."
- **Profile clicks**: "A sign of interest. People want to look into your account."
- **Media engagements**: "A sign of approval. Viewers want to see more of your content."

## Instagram Metrics

### Core Metrics

| Metric | Definition | What It Indicates |
|--------|------------|-------------------|
| **Reach** | Unique accounts that saw content | Audience size |
| **Impressions** | Total times content was viewed | Stickiness (multiple views) |
| **Likes** | Heart reactions | Basic engagement |
| **Comments** | Text responses | Conversation level |
| **Shares** | DM shares or Story shares | Personal recommendations |
| **Saves** | Bookmarks for later | **MOST IMPORTANT** - indicates value |
| **Profile Visits** | Clicks to your profile | Brand interest |
| **External Link Taps** | Bio link clicks | CTA conversion |
| **Average Watch Time** | For video/Reels | Content engagement quality |

### Instagram Algorithm Signals (Critical!)
- **Likes**: 1x weight
- **Comments**: 4x weight
- **Saves**: 5x weight (**strongest signal**)
- **Shares**: 6x weight

### What Metric Combinations Mean on Instagram
- **High saves**: Content is educational/actionable/reference-worthy
- **High comments**: Content sparked conversation
- **High shares**: Content is worth recommending to friends
- **High likes, low saves**: Content is entertaining but not valuable
- **High reach from non-followers**: Content is being discovered (hashtags, Explore)
- **High completion rate (video)**: Content is engaging throughout

### Instagram Benchmarks
- Good engagement rate: 1-3%
- Typical reach: ~20% of followers
- Excellent save rate: >8%
- Strong share rate: >5%

### Key Insight from Instagram Head Adam Mosseri
> For growth, prioritize monitoring:
> 1. **Average watch time** (for video)
> 2. **Like rate**
> 3. **Send rate** (shares via DM)

### Instagram-Specific Guidance
- Saves are the strongest algorithm signal
- Reels have higher discovery potential than feed posts
- Carousel posts get higher save rates (educational content)
- Reply to comments to boost post visibility
- Use 3-5 relevant hashtags, not 30

## Engagement Patterns to Detect

### High-Performing Patterns
1. **Conversation Starter**: High comment ratio (>30% of total engagement)
2. **Viral Potential**: High share ratio (>25% of total engagement)
3. **Reference Content**: High save ratio (Instagram, >10%)
4. **Action Driver**: High click rate (>2% CTR)
5. **Algorithm Boost**: Impressions >> expected reach

### Poor-Performing Patterns
1. **Passive Engagement**: >90% of engagement is just likes
2. **High Reach, Low Engagement**: Content disappoints after hook
3. **Ghost Content**: Impressions > 100 but zero engagement
4. **Weak CTA**: Low clicks despite high engagement

## Implementing These Insights

The `stats-reviewer.ts` service implements all of this research:

1. **`analyzePostMetrics()`**: Interprets each metric with platform-specific context
2. **`detectEngagementPatterns()`**: Identifies success/failure patterns
3. **`generateLearningPrompt()`**: Creates AI-friendly summaries of what worked/didn't
4. **`getPlatformMetricGuide()`**: Returns platform-specific guidance for content creation

### Example Usage

```typescript
import { analyzePostMetrics, generateLearningPrompt } from './lib/learning/stats-reviewer';

const analysis = analyzePostMetrics('linkedin', {
  impressions: 5000,
  likes: 150,
  comments: 45,
  shares: 20,
  clicks: 30,
  engagementRate: 4.9,
});

// Get AI-friendly summary
const learningPrompt = generateLearningPrompt(analysis);

// Detected patterns and insights
console.log(analysis.patterns);
console.log(analysis.keyInsights);
console.log(analysis.recommendations);
```
